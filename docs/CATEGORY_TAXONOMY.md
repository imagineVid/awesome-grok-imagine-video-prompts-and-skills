# Grok Imagine Video Workflow Taxonomy

This collection classifies a prompt by the production problem it solves, not by a loose visual genre. Every community case receives one primary workflow so readers can compare prompts with the same directing intent.

## Camera Direction & Shot Design

Use this group when the prompt is mainly a shot plan: framing, lens feel, camera path, blocking, pacing, reveal, or transition. A beautiful scene with no meaningful camera instruction belongs elsewhere.

## Dialogue, Performance & Native Audio

Use this group when speech, acting, ambience, music, or synchronized sound is essential to the result. The record should preserve the exact spoken line and identify the sound cues that need to land on visible action.

## Product Motion & Commercial Spots

Use this group for product reveals, food and beverage motion, fashion clips, app/device demonstrations, branded social creative, and short advertisements. The product or offer must remain the narrative center of the clip.

## Image-to-Video & Subject Continuity

Use this group when a source frame anchors identity, composition, garment, object design, storyboard geometry, or environment layout. Reviewers should record what must remain stable and what motion is allowed to change.

## Stylized Motion & Visual Effects

Use this group for animation language, simulation, transformations, surreal physics, title sequences, retro media treatments, and effects-led shots. The motion system or visual treatment should be reusable beyond one subject.

## Video Editing, Restyling & Scene Control

Use this group when the workflow begins with an existing clip and asks Grok Imagine to add, remove, replace, restyle, extend, or redirect part of the scene. Describe both the requested change and the continuity constraints.

## Classification Check

Before assigning a workflow, ask:

1. What input does the prompt assume: text, a still image, or an existing video?
2. What is the main production challenge: camera, performance/audio, product communication, continuity, effects, or editing?
3. Which instruction would make the result stop being the same workflow if removed?

That answer determines the primary category. Secondary tags may be added later, but they must not replace one clear editorial decision.
