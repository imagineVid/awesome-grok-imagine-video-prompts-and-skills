# Contributing a Grok Imagine Video Case

The collection publishes evidence-backed video workflows, not prompt-shaped marketing copy. A useful submission lets another creator understand the shot, verify the model, inspect the result, and adapt the direction.

## Evidence Bundle

Open the **Submit a Prompt** issue and include:

- a concise case title and one-sentence production purpose;
- the complete prompt, including dialogue, camera, timing, audio, and continuity instructions that were actually used;
- the canonical public source URL and original creator attribution;
- direct video media, an embeddable preview, or a source post where the result can be played;
- the input mode: text-to-video, image-to-video, video editing, or extension;
- explicit evidence that Grok Imagine generated or edited the clip;
- the most relevant workflow from `docs/CATEGORY_TAXONOMY.md`.

## Model Scope

Current records may target `grok-imagine-video` or `grok-imagine-video-1.5`, but the exact model/version must be preserved when the source provides it. Do not relabel output from another video model just because the prompt could also work in Grok Imagine.

## Editorial Review

A maintainer checks:

1. **Traceability**: source, creator, result, and model claim can be verified.
2. **Prompt integrity**: the submitted text is complete or any reconstruction is labeled honestly.
3. **Production value**: the case teaches a reusable directing pattern rather than presenting a vague aesthetic.
4. **Video evidence**: the media proves motion, timing, continuity, or audio behavior; a disconnected still is insufficient.
5. **Uniqueness**: normalized prompt text, source post, video URL, and perceptual previews do not duplicate an existing case.
6. **Rights**: attribution is retained and any reuse terms are respected.

Approval is an editorial decision. Engagement helps discovery, but it never overrides missing evidence.

## Corrections and Removal

Creators and rights holders may open an issue with the affected record and requested action. Verified attribution corrections and removal requests receive priority over ordinary additions.
